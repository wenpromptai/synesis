# CPO Adoption Curve ↔ Valuation Disconnect

## Abstract

The market is pricing optical/CPO stocks on 2028–2030 TAM penetration rates ($90B AI optics by 2028, 30% CPO penetration by 2029), but CPO volume production doesn't begin until 2027–2028 and initially replaces copper — not existing pluggable fiber. This creates a 12–18 month "faith gap" where valuations are detached from near-term revenue, making the entire sector vulnerable to any delay in the adoption timeline.

## Why This Pattern Matters

1. **Reflexive pricing**: LITE's S&P 500 inclusion creates forced passive buying → higher market cap → more index weight → more buying. Price becomes self-reinforcing, independent of fundamentals.
2. **TAM-based valuation**: Market using $90B 2028 TAM, but CPO penetration at 4% in 2026 means current revenue is from pluggable (not CPO). The CPO premium is entirely forward-looking.
3. **Adoption delay risk**: If CPO volume slips from 2027 to 2028-2029 (manufacturing readiness, thermal/reliability challenges), the "faith gap" widens and valuations compress.
4. **Sector correlation on downside**: All names sold off together on profit-taking days (March 3-4, Q1 end March 31). Tariff catalysts (Section 232, April 14) hit the entire stack simultaneously.

## Implication

Treat the optical/CPO sector as a single correlated risk cluster. Sizing should account for portfolio-level exposure across all names. The highest-conviction trade is identifying which companies benefit from BOTH pluggable (now) AND CPO (future) rather than betting purely on CPO timing. [[tickers/MTSI]] and [[tickers/COHR]] have more dual-exposure; [[tickers/LITE]] is more levered to pluggable EML dominance.
