# Insider Selling Cluster — Cross-Sector Signal (Apr 2026)

> [!abstract]
> Six companies across three sectors (optics, tech, fintech) showed coordinated C-suite/director selling with near-zero offsetting insider buys during Q1 2026. The cross-sector breadth of this signal distinguishes it from sector-specific profit-taking — it suggests broad management skepticism about forward valuations regardless of the growth narrative.

## Why This Pattern Matters

1. **Cross-sector breadth**: Three optics names + one mega-cap + one fintech + one semi. This isn't a single-sector phenomenon.
2. **Selling into strength**: All six were near or at multi-month highs when insiders sold. The absence of insider buys across the entire group is notable.
3. **Compound risk in optics**: AAOI, FN, and LPTH overlap with the [[themes/working-capital-deterioration]] theme — insiders selling while cash conversion is deteriorating is a particularly bearish signal.
4. **Trader response**: The 2026-04-10 brief Trader passed on most of these names or took defined-risk bearish structures, citing insider selling as a consistent bear factor.

## Implication

Monitor Form 4 filings across this group as a cohort. If selling accelerates while stock prices remain elevated, the probability of a coordinated rerating event increases. Conversely, any insider *buying* would be a strong contrarian signal.
