# AI Optics Names with Customer Concentration AND Working Capital Deterioration

> [!abstract]
> Four datacenter optics companies share a compound risk: extreme customer concentration alongside deteriorating cash conversion. This combination is particularly dangerous because a single customer pause simultaneously hits revenue AND triggers working capital unwinds (AR write-offs, inventory obsolescence).

## Nodes Involved

- [[tickers/AAOI]] — Digicomm 53.1%, MSFT 28.8% of rev (FY2025 10-K); AR doubled to $244.4M; OCF -$174.4M
- [[tickers/FN]] — 4 customers = 59.1% of rev (Q2 FY2026); CFO $46.3M vs NI $112.6M; inventory $798.9M
- [[tickers/COHR]] — Cash conversion deteriorated ~5 quarters; inventory/AR bloated; net debt ~$2.49B
- [[tickers/LITE]] — Working capital -$1.48B; current debt $3.24B; D/E 392; EV/EBITDA ~247x

## Evidence

All four sit in the [[themes/optical-photonics-supply-chain]] serving the 800G-to-1.6T datacenter bandwidth transition. Despite strong revenue growth narratives, each shows the same pattern: AR and inventory growing faster than revenue, converting reported profits into negative or fragile FCF.


## Implications

- These four are correlated on the downside — a single hyperscaler order pause or optics inventory correction would hit all simultaneously
- Monitor as a cluster: if one reports inventory write-downs or AR provisions, the others are likely exposed
