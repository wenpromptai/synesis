# AI Infrastructure Financing Risk

The hyperscaler AI capex cycle is increasingly funded through intermediaries (CoreWeave, etc.) using leveraged structures (convertible notes, project finance). If credit tightens — oil-driven inflation re-acceleration → higher rates → tighter financial conditions — the AI capex chain becomes fragile. A CoreWeave credit event would cascade through GPU demand (NVDA), interconnect (MRVL), and optics.

## Key Signal: CoreWeave + Meta $35B (2026-04-09)

- CoreWeave signed a NEW $21B deal with Meta, bringing total contracts to **$35B through 2032** (2026-04-09, Bloomberg/CNBC)
- New capacity will use NVIDIA Rubin chips
- CoreWeave proposed a **$3B convertible notes offering** to fund expansion
- CoreWeave also has commitments to Google, Microsoft, and OpenAI

## Why This Matters

1. **Leverage concentration**: CoreWeave is a single point of failure with $35B+ in commitments funded by debt/converts, not operating cash flow
2. **Macro feedback loop**: Oil >$100 → inflation → higher rates → tighter credit → harder for CoreWeave to refinance → demand risk for NVDA/MRVL
4. **Contrast with direct builders**: Meta chose CoreWeave (OPEX) over building in-house (CAPEX). If CoreWeave stumbles, Meta's AI training timeline slips.

- CoreWeave credit spreads / convertible note pricing
