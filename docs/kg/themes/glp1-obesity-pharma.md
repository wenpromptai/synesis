# GLP-1 / Obesity Pharma Structural Growth

GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) have shifted the obesity treatment paradigm from niche to mainstream. Multi-year compounding demand driven by market penetration (~15-20% addressable globally vs low single-digit current penetration), obesity comorbidity treatment adjacency (heart disease, NASH, CKD), and ongoing pipeline expansion into Alzheimer's, muscle preservation, and addiction.

## Why Healthcare Works in This Macro

1. **Non-cyclical demand:** GLP-1 prescriptions continue regardless of macro regime; patients maintain therapy for multi-year periods.
2. **Defensive + growth barbell:** In a sticky-rate environment where long-duration SaaS is penalized, high-FCF pharma compounders offer quality growth without duration risk.
3. **Pipeline optionality:** Each new indication (heart, kidney, Alzheimer's) expands TAM without requiring a new molecule — amortizes R&D spend over larger markets.

## Risks
- **LOE (Loss of Exclusivity):** Patent cliffs begin ~2031-2033; biosimilar competition will compress margins.
- **Supply constraints:** LLY and NVO are capacity-constrained; manufacturing ramp failure = missed revenue.
- **Pricing pressure:** US drug pricing reform risk; international reference pricing compresses realized ASPs.
- **Safety headline:** Any unexpected adverse events (muscle loss, pancreatitis, thyroid) trigger sharp re-rating.
