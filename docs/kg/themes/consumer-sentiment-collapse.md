# Consumer Sentiment Collapse

University of Michigan consumer sentiment hit a **record low of 47.6** in April 2026 (est 51.5, prev 57.0) — the worst reading ever, below the 1980 energy crisis low of 51.7. One-year inflation expectations spiked to **4.8%** (up 100bps in a single month, largest jump since April 2025). 98% of interviews were conducted before the ceasefire announcement.

## Key Data Points (2026-04-11)
| Indicator | Value | Signal |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| UMich Sentiment | 47.6 | Record low — below 1980 energy crisis |
| 1Y Inflation Expectations | 4.8% | +100bps in one month, unanchoring risk |
| Personal Savings Rate | 4.0% | Down from 4.5%, consumer cushion thinning |
| CPI YoY (headline) | 3.3% | Hot (energy-driven) |
| Gasoline CPI MoM | +21.2% | Largest on record since 1967 |
| Energy CPI MoM | +10.9% | Largest since Sept 2005 |

## Market Disconnect
The market is rallying (S&P 500 best week since November, VIX 25.78 -> 19.49) while consumers feel the worst they've ever felt. This divergence historically resolves with either:
1. Sentiment catching up to markets via lower energy prices (if ceasefire holds), or
2. Markets catching down to sentiment via weak earnings/spending data

## Contrarian View
The UMich number may be backward-looking — 98% of interviews were pre-ceasefire. If ceasefire holds and gas retreats, May preliminary read could be dramatically better. However, if it doesn't, Q1 earnings will reveal the spending pullback.

- Q1 retail sales and consumer spending data
- Bank earnings (GS, JPM week of 4/13) for consumer credit quality
