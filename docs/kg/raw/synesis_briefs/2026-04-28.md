Commodities signaled geopolitics (USO +1.8%) amid headlines on the Strait of Hormuz and stalled US-Iran negotiations; that raises tail risk even as financial conditions remain loose (NFCI ~ -0.50) and credit spreads stay tight (HY OAS ~2.86%). Geopolitics (Hormuz) scenarios:
1) De-escalation / partial reopening (55%): oil gives back some risk premium → equities stabilize; rate pressure eases modestly; credit stays tight.
2) Prolonged standoff / periodic disruptions (35%): oil stays bid → inflation risk premium persists; duration remains weak; cyclicals vs defensives dispersion rises.
3) Acute disruption / military escalation (10%): oil spikes → financial conditions tighten; HY spreads widen; equity drawdown led by discretionary/transport; gold rallies sharply.
