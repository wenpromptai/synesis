## Macro Regime: Transitioning (+0.10)

The regime remains **transitioning** but with an improving bias vs. last week. Two competing forces:

**Positive:** Core CPI printed below expectations (2.6% YoY vs 2.7% est, +0.2% MoM vs 0.3% est) — the underlying inflation picture ex-energy is cooling. **The disconnect:** The market is rallying hard while consumers feel the worst they've ever felt. This divergence historically resolves with either (a) sentiment catching up to markets via lower energy prices, or (b) markets catching down to sentiment via weak earnings/spending data. Q1 earnings season starting Monday (GS, JPM, NFLX, TSM) will be the arbiter.

## Contrarian Takes

1. **The insider selling cluster is less alarming than it looks.** In a parabolic market, insiders always sell — it's rational portfolio management, not a bearish signal. The absence of buying is the real tell, but in a >$100 stock universe, insider buys are rare anyway. Don't let this override the fundamental thesis.

2. **Ceasefire is more likely to hold than the market thinks.** Iran's internal power struggle (IRGC vs. Ghalibaf/Araghchi) makes escalation harder, not easier — no one has authority to resume hostilities unilaterally. Japan SPR release + India's Russian crude imports provide supply buffer. Oil at $80–85 by end of April if Hormuz opens gradually.

3. **The UMich number is backward-looking.** 98% of interviews were pre-ceasefire. April preliminary sentiment was measured while gas was at peak and the war was in full swing. The May read will be dramatically better if ceasefire holds and gas retreats.

4. **AI infrastructure is becoming anti-fragile.** CoreWeave's $35B in contracts, LITE's 2028 backlog, and NVIDIA's $2B Lumentum lock-in are creating a flywheel where each commitment validates and de-risks the next. The bear case requires credit to tighten enough to break the entire chain — possible but increasingly improbable as revenue starts flowing.
