# BRK.B — Berkshire Hathaway (Class B)

**First appeared: 2026-05-03 (risk_on regime)**

## 2026-05-03 — Q1 2026 earnings + record cash
- Q1 2026 operating earnings: **~$11.35B (+18% y/y)** — strong diversified performance.
- Cash + T-bills: **~$397B (record high)** — Buffett sitting on the largest cash pile in history.
- Bull: Buffett's record cash pile is a call option on a drawdown. BRK.B can deploy at scale into a correction, compounding the quality-balance-sheet bid. Diversified cash flows (insurance, utilities, railroads, equities) insulate from single-factor risk.
- Bear: Opportunity cost of holding $397B cash in low-yield environment while equities rally; T-bill income does not replace equity upside. Succession risk (Buffett/Munger era).
- Source: [[sources/brief-2026-05-03]]

## Key Thesis
**Regime fit:** Works best as a "quality defensive with optionality" — in a transitioning/risk_on regime, BRK.B is a way to own market exposure without single-sector concentration. The record cash signals Buffett sees few cheap opportunities, which is itself a late-cycle signal.

**Contrarian read:** If markets correct 15-20%, BRK.B's $397B deployment capacity makes it uniquely positioned to buy the dip at scale. This creates an asymmetric floor vs pure equity beta names. In a [[themes/stagflation-tail-risk]] environment, BRK.B's diversified non-cyclical cash flows (insurance, utilities, railroads) insulate from single-sector risk, while the record cash pile preserves optionality to deploy into dislocated assets.
